# X / Twitter Platform Rules

## Format

- **Character limit:** 280 characters maximum
- **Line breaks:** Use them. Short lines. White space breathes.
- **No hashtags** in Phase 1 — they signal marketing, not authentic voice
- **No emojis** unless they are doing specific emotional work (rarely)
- **One idea per post** — never try to say two things

## Audience on X

Advocates, therapists, policy-adjacent people, people who have personally experienced the access crisis, journalists covering mental health. They are sophisticated. They have seen a lot of wellness content. They are skeptical of brands.

## What Performs on X for This Audience

- Specific human stories (real or composite — never fabricated)
- Statistics made visceral through human scale
- Pattern observations that feel true the moment you read them
- Quiet conviction, not performative outrage
- Things that make people stop and think "I've seen that"

## What Does Not Perform

- Thesis statements that can be paraphrased
- Lists ("3 reasons why...")
- Corporate advocacy ("We believe in...")
- Abstract calls to action

## The Image-First Opening Rule

See [[content/hooks.md]] — this is the most important rule for X posts. Every post must open with a specific physical image, not a thesis.

## Tone on X Specifically

Advocacy voice. Founder-adjacent. Personal. The posts should feel like they could have come from a clinician who has been sitting with this problem for 20 years and can no longer stay quiet.

## Phase 1 Constraint

**Do not mention iVASA in the post body.** We are in problem awareness mode. The audience needs to recognize the crisis before we introduce the solution. iVASA lives in the bio and the pinned post.
