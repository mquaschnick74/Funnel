# The Therapy Desert Audience

## The Core Statistic

158 million Americans live in therapy deserts — geographic areas with critically insufficient mental health providers relative to population need.

## What "Therapy Desert" Actually Means

- Waiting lists of 3-6 months for an initial appointment
- Driving 2-3 hours each way to see a provider
- "Not accepting new patients" as the first thing they hear
- Telehealth that exists in theory but requires stable internet and a private space
- Cost: $150-$300 per session without insurance
- Insurance coverage that lists providers who are no longer practicing or not accepting patients

## The Human Experience

This is not an abstract policy problem. It is:
- The person who finally decided to ask for help — and couldn't get it
- The parent watching their teenager deteriorate on a 4-month waitlist
- The rural community where the only psychiatrist retired and no one replaced them
- The person who "should be fine" because they have insurance — and still can't get an appointment

## Emotional Texture of the Content

Posts about therapy deserts should carry:
- Quiet grief, not outrage
- Specificity — one person, one moment, not a statistic
- The weight of accumulated waiting
- The dignity of people who are trying to get help and being turned away

## What the Audience Recognizes

People in therapy deserts know this experience viscerally. The goal is recognition — "that's me" or "that's someone I love." Not education. Not persuasion. Recognition.

## Statistics to Use Authentically

- 158 million Americans in therapy deserts
- 60% of counties in the US have no psychiatrists at all
- Average wait time for mental health care: 25 days (in areas that have providers)
- Cost of traditional therapy: $150-$300/session without insurance
