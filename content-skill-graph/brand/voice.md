# Brand Voice

## Core Descriptors

**Warm.** We care about the person reading this. That warmth is never saccharine or performed — it is grounded in genuine concern for people who cannot get help.

**Sophisticated.** We speak to intelligent adults. We do not over-explain. We do not condescend. We trust the reader.

**Grounded.** We are not aspirational wellness culture. We are rooted in clinical reality — 20 years of practice, real people, real suffering, real patterns.

**Empowering.** We do not make people feel broken. We make them feel seen — and capable of going deeper.

## What This Sounds Like

- Like a wise guide, not a sterile tool
- Direct language, no jargon
- First person plural when speaking as the company ("We")
- Never corporate. Never generic wellness. Never Silicon Valley hype.

## What This Does NOT Sound Like

- "Unlock your potential" — generic self-help noise
- "Breakthrough" / "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing" — hollow enthusiasm
- "We're here for you" — too soft, too passive
- Anything that sounds like it was written by a marketing department

## Tone Calibration by Situation

| Post Type | Tone Modifier |
|-----------|--------------|
| Access crisis / therapy desert | Quiet urgency. Grief-adjacent. Human scale. |
| Pattern insight | Curious, slightly unsettling, precise |
| Founder voice | Personal, direct, a little raw |
| Advocacy | Conviction without rage |

## The Test

Read the post aloud. Does it sound like a person who has spent 20 years in a therapy room and genuinely cares? If yes — proceed. If it sounds like a brand — rewrite.
